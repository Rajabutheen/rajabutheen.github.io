Roland Garros - Tennis Grand Slam

Reimagining the game of Tennis through data.

  •   Duration

    5 months

  •   Domain

    Sports Technology

  •   Role

    UX Design, UI Design

OVERVIEW

Roland Garros is an annual tennis tournament held in Paris. It is one of the four tournaments that make up the Grand Slam of tennis. It has started over a century ago and over time has emerged to be the premier tennis tournament and the only among the Grand Slam tournaments to be played on clay.

As a UI UX designer, I'm committed to enhancing the experience of the Match Centre Application, It is a Live Interactive Tennis data visualization app that helps the users experience the game immersively. The app uses data to represent stats in graphical and pictorial form in 2D and 3D, this helps to measure player performance and gain a competitive sports advantage.

PROBLEM

Problem statement

Tennis enthusiasts use the Match Centre application to learn about the Grandslam tournament matches and their statistics. Users find this application hard to interpret as the data is not communicative. This causes frustration for the users and leads to a higher bounce rate. As well most of the features are left unexplored.

Discovery of the problem

The telemetry data we got from the analytics tool for the previous year's tournament made us look for the problems involved in it and paved the path to creating new opportunities. The KPI metrics that we have defined have helped us establish a clear connection between the users and the product. We have defined the most important metrics for our website like Avg. session duration, Unique visitors, Bounce rate, Page views, and some more.

Validating the problem

Primary users

Fans

Players

Coaches

Bloggers

User Interview

To understand the problem better we have decided to conduct Interview with the existing users. we asked about their thoughts on the features, and the difficulty level.

Analysing the problem

Affinity Map

An affinity map helped to group similar observations. I used it to surface several key needs and pains common amongst participants. That made us move forward for the prioritization easily.

2x2 Matrix

Another tool we utilized was 2x2 matrices. They help us better understand the relationships between two things on a spectrum. We used it in my research findings to map needs and pains important to users versus business. It also helped us prioritize which points to tackle first.

The persona of a typical user

I created user personas to put a face on the target users and visualize various aspects of their behavior and motivations.

IDEATION

Information Architecture

The newly designed IA increases the visibility of the app’s key features and improves the overall organization and navigation throughout the app.

Wireframe

We get our research and learnings to help direct a solution that aims to solve problems and achieve our business goals. I have created the Hi-Fi wireframes and that were presented for review and design iterations.

AB testing

I have used this technique to show two or more variants of the designs to stakeholders and Users at random to find out which one performs better. The concepts were voted purely in terms of understanding the data and ease of use. Based on the results we have gone ahead with Variant 2 for our key Matchbeats feature.

Feature breakdown

I have designed new elements with the ability to achieve the task easily and efficiently. Hierarchy, Visual cues, Prominence, and Intuitive interactions were considered as part of the solution.
